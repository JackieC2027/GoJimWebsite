Triceps Exercises

Tricep Extension

  • Stand facing the cable machine with your feet shoulder-width apart and your back straight.
  • Grasp the cable attachment with an overhand grip, keeping your elbows close to your sides.
  • Exhale and push the attachment down towards your thighs, extending your arms fully.
  • Pause briefly at the end of the movement, then slowly release the attachment back up to the starting position, inhaling as you lift.
  • Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  • Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
  • Vary the attachment and grip position to target different areas of your triceps.
  • Increase the weight gradually over time as you get stronger.
  • Use a spotter or safety rails if necessary, especially when lifting heavy weights.

    • Tricep Dips

    1. Position yourself between two parallel bars with your hands gripping each bar, shoulder-width apart.
    2. Extend your legs out in front of you, keeping your feet together and your heels on the ground.
    3. Lower your body by bending your elbows, keeping your elbows close to your sides and your shoulders down.
    4. Pause briefly at the bottom of the movement, then push your body back up to the starting position by straightening your arms, exhaling as you lift.
    5. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
    6. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
    7. Vary the width of your grip to target different areas of your triceps.
    8. Increase the number of reps gradually over time as you get stronger.
    9. Use a spotter or safety rails if necessary, especially when attempting more challenging variations.

    Close-Grip Bench Press

    1. Lie on a flat bench with your feet flat on the ground and your back arched.
    2. Grip the barbell with your hands positioned shoulder-width apart, keeping your elbows close to your sides.
    3. Exhale and lift the barbell off the rack, keeping it directly above your chest.
    4. Lower the barbell towards your chest, keeping your elbows close to your sides and your shoulders down.
    5. Pause briefly at the bottom of the movement, then push the barbell back up to the starting position, exhaling as you lift.
    6. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
    7. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
    8. Vary your grip width to target different areas of your triceps.
    9. Increase the weight gradually over time as you get stronger.
    10. Use a spotter or safety rails if necessary, especially when lifting heavy weights.

    Diamond Push-Ups

    1. Get into a push-up position on the floor, with your hands close together and your thumbs and index fingers touching to form a diamond shape.
    2. Keep your feet together and your back straight, with your core engaged.
    3. Lower your body towards the ground by bending your elbows, keeping them close to your sides.
    4. Pause briefly at the bottom of the movement, then push your body back up to the starting position, exhaling as you lift.
    5. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
    6. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
    7. Modify the exercise by doing it on your knees or against a wall if necessary.
    8. Increase the number of reps gradually over time as you get stronger.
    9. Use a spotter or safety rails if necessary, especially when attempting more challenging variations.

    Cable Tricep Overhead Extensions

    1. Attach a rope or a straight bar to a high pulley cable and stand facing away from the machine with your feet shoulder-width apart.
    2. Hold the rope or bar with an overhand grip and extend your arms straight up above your head.
    3. Lower the rope or bar behind your head, bending your elbows and keeping your upper arms close to your head.
    4. Pause briefly at the bottom of the movement, then lift the rope or bar back up to the starting position, exhaling as you lift.
    5. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
    6. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
    7. Vary the attachment and grip position to target different areas of your triceps.
    8. Increase the weight gradually over time as you get stronger.
    9. Use a spotter or safety rails if necessary, especially when lifting heavy weights.
