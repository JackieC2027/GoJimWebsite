Chest Exercises

Barbell Bench

// gif of demostration // tips on form in a organized list

  1. Lie on the bench with your feet flat on the floor and your eyes directly under the barbell.
  2. Grip the bar with your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart and lift it off the rack.
  3. Lower the bar towards your chest, keeping your elbows close to your body.
  4. Pause briefly at the bottom of the movement, then press the bar back up to the starting position.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  6. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back flat on the bench and your feet planted on the floor.
  7. Increase the weight gradually over time as you get stronger.

Incline Dumbbell Press

// gif of demostration // tips on form in a organized list

  1. Adjust the incline bench to a 30 to 45-degree angle and position yourself comfortably.
  2. Hold the dumbbells with an overhand grip and let your arms hang straight down.
  3. Lower the dumbbells towards your shoulders, keeping your elbows close to your body.
  4. Pause briefly at the bottom of the movement, then press the dumbbells back up to the starting position.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  6. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back flat on the bench and your feet planted on the floor.
  7. Increase the weight gradually over time as you get stronger.

Cable Chest Flys

  1. Adjust the cable machine so that the handles are at chest height.
  2. Stand facing away from the machine and grasp the handles with an overhand grip.
  3. Step forward to create tension on the cables, with one foot slightly in front of the other.
  4. Slowly bring your arms forward and together in front of your chest, keeping your elbows slightly bent.
  5. Pause briefly at the end of the movement, then slowly release your arms back to the starting position.
  6. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  7. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your shoulders down and your core engaged.
  8. Increase the weight gradually over time as you get stronger.

Chest Dips

  1. Stand between the parallel bars and place your hands on the bars with your palms facing in.
  2. Jump up and straighten your arms, supporting your bodyweight with your arms and shoulders.
  3. Lower your body by bending your elbows and leaning forward, keeping your elbows close to your sides.
  4. Lower your body until your shoulders are at the same height as your elbows or lower.
  5. Push yourself back up to the starting position by straightening your arms, exhaling as you lift.
  6. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  7. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your shoulders down and your chest up.
  8. If necessary, use a spotter or assistance machine to help you complete the exercise with proper form.

Pec Deck

  1. Adjust the seat height so that your elbows are level with the handles.
  2. Sit with your back against the pad and grasp the handles with an overhand grip.
  3. Bring the handles towards the center of your chest, squeezing your chest muscles together.
  4. Pause briefly at the end of the movement, then slowly release the handles back to the starting position.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  6. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back against the pad and your feet firmly on the ground.
  7. Increase the weight gradually over time as you get stronger.
