Bicep Exercises

Bicep Curl

// gif of demostration

// tips on form in a organized list

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding the dumbbells in each hand with your palms facing forward.
  2. Keep your elbows close to your body and curl the dumbbells up towards your shoulders, exhaling as you lift.
  3. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, squeezing your bicep muscles.
  4. Lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position, inhaling as you lower.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  6. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, avoid swinging or using momentum to lift the weight.
  7. Increase the weight gradually over time as you get stronger.

Preacher Curl

  1. Adjust the preacher bench to fit your height and position yourself comfortably.
  2. Hold the barbell with an underhand grip and rest your upper arms on the bench.
  3. Curl the weight towards your shoulders, exhaling as you lift.
  4. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, squeezing your bicep muscles.
  5. Lower the weight back down to the starting position, inhaling as you lower.
  6. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  7. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, avoid swinging or using momentum to lift the weight.
  8. Increase the weight gradually over time as you get stronger.

Incline Curls

  1. Adjust the incline bench to a 45-degree angle and position yourself comfortably.
  2. Hold the dumbbells with an underhand grip and let your arms hang straight down.
  3. Curl the weights towards your shoulders, exhaling as you lift.
  4. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, squeezing your bicep muscles.
  5. Lower the weights back down to the starting position, inhaling as you lower.
  6. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  7. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, avoid swinging or using momentum to lift the weight.
  8. Increase the weight gradually over time as you get stronger.

Hammer Curls

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold the dumbbells at your sides with a neutral grip (palms facing in).
  2. Curl the weights towards your shoulders, keeping your elbows close to your body.
  3. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, squeezing your bicep muscles.
  4. Lower the weights back down to the starting position, inhaling as you lower.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  6. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, avoid swinging or using momentum to lift the weight.
  7. Increase the weight gradually over time as you get stronger.
